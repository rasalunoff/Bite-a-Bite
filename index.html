01 🔍
Discovery & Opportunity
Validate the gap. Know the market.
Week 1–2
▼
💡
Your Opportunity: Kerala snacks market is underserved in frozen/RTC format. iD Fresh & MTR focus on breakfast items — not snacks. No major brand owns authentic Kerala snacks. Bite a Bite can own this niche first.
Action Steps
  • 1
    Survey 50 people in Kottakkal area
    Ask: Do you buy frozen snacks? What brand? What would you pay for authentic Kerala samosa/spring roll pack of 10? Use WhatsApp or in-person. Takes 2 days.
  • 2
    Visit 5 local supermarkets & kirana stores
    Check what frozen snack brands are stocked, at what price, how much shelf space they get. Photograph everything. This is your real competitive intel.
  • 3
    Identify your "unfair advantage"
    Your edge: no preservatives + authentic Kerala recipe + Halal + reasonable price. Write this as a 1-line pitch: "Bite a Bite — Real Kerala taste, no preservatives, ready in 5 minutes."
  • 4
    Check iD Fresh, MTR, Haldiram's prices at nearby stores
    Note pack size, price, SKUs available. This will directly anchor your pricing decision.
✅
Validation signal to look for: If 30+ out of 50 people say "Yes I'd pay ₹80–₹120 for a pack of 10 authentic Kerala snacks," you have a viable market. Proceed.

Purpose & Product Strategy
Your mission, UVP, and business model.
Week 1–2
▼

Suggested Mission: "To make authentic Kerala flavours accessible to every busy household — no preservatives, no compromise on taste."

    Write your business plan (1-pager)
    Product list, target customer, pricing, channels, costs, revenue targets. Needed for bank account, distributor conversations, and to keep your co-founders aligned.
03 🏭
Production Unit Setup
Your kitchen-to-factory in Kottakkal.
Week 1–6 ★ Critical
▼
⚠️
This is your longest phase. Blast freezer procurement, FSSAI approval, and pilot batches take 4–6 weeks minimum. Start Day 1. Everything else can run in parallel.
Licenses — Priority Order
  • 1
    FSSAI Basic Registration (₹100/year)
    Apply online at foscos.fssai.gov.in. For turnover under ₹12L/year. Takes 7–30 days. Do this FIRST — you cannot sell food without it.
  • 2
    GST Registration (Free)
    Mandatory if you sell online or interstate. Apply at gst.gov.in. Takes 3–7 working days. Required for Amazon, Flipkart, and B2B invoicing.
  • 3
    MSME / Udyam Registration (Free)
    Register at udyamregistration.gov.in. Gives you access to government schemes, subsidised loans, and priority lending. 10-minute process.
  • 4
    Shop & Establishment License (State)
    Apply at Kerala Labour Dept. Required if you have employees or a commercial premise. ₹500–₹2,000 fee depending on employee count.
  • 5
    Halal Certification
    Apply with IHCA (Islamic Halal Certification Agency) or HFSAA. Essential for your Muslim customer base in Kerala. Takes 2–4 weeks. Annual renewal. Cost ₹5,000–₹15,000.
Equipment & Setup
  • 6
    Buy a Blast Freezer (₹80,000–₹1,50,000)
    This is your most critical equipment. A blast freezer freezes product core to -18°C rapidly — essential for food safety and shelf life without preservatives. Buy 200–300L capacity to start. Look at Blue Star, Voltas, or commercial suppliers in Kozhikode.
  • 7
    Setup dedicated production space
    Separate from your personal kitchen. Can be a room in the same building. Needs: stainless steel prep table, separate handwash sink, proper ventilation, pest-proof storage. FSSAI will inspect this.
  • 8
    Finalise raw material suppliers
    Vegetables + chicken: local wholesale market Kottakkal/Malappuram. Packaging (pouches, labels): Bangalore or Kozhikode packaging suppliers. Spices and batter mix: Identify a consistent supplier — taste consistency depends on it.
  • 9
    Write your SOPs for each product
    Recipe card + step-by-step production process + quality checkpoints (raw material check, weight per piece, freeze temp, packaging seal check). This ensures consistency when you hire staff.
  • 10
    Run 3 pilot batches before launch
    Batch 1: Recipe finalisation. Batch 2: Packaging trial. Batch 3: Shelf-life test (freeze, store 30 days, cook and taste). Only launch after Batch 3 passes your quality bar.

05 🎨
Branding, Design & Packaging
Make Bite a Bite unforgettable.
Week 2–4
▼
🎨
Logo Feedback: Your "Bite a Bite" logo is fun, bold, and memorable — the bubbly orange font with the bite-mark detail is excellent. It communicates playful, food-forward, and friendly. Keep it. It works especially well on packaging and social media.
Brand Identity Guidelines
  • 1
    Primary Color: Bite Orange (#E85A1A)
    Your logo orange is perfect. Extend it consistently to all packaging, social posts, website, and print. Secondary: warm cream (#FFF4EC) for backgrounds. Accent: deep brown (#1C1008) for text.
  • 2
    Typography: Keep the bubbly logo font + clean body font
    For social media and marketing headlines: Nunito Black or Baloo 2 (Google Fonts, free). For body copy and labels: Plus Jakarta Sans or DM Sans. Avoid serif fonts — they feel wrong for a playful food brand.
  • 3
    Brand Voice: Warm, playful, authentic Kerala pride
    Talk like a proud Malayali amma who cooks well. Phrases: "Homemade taste, zero compromise." / "Veetile ruchi, anywhere you are." Mix Malayalam taglines with English for local connection.
  • 4
    Packaging Design — Zipper Pouch
    Front: Product photo (hero shot of cooked snack), Bite a Bite logo top-left, product name bold center, "No Preservatives" badge, Halal symbol, FSSAI license number. Back: Cooking instructions (Malayalam + English), nutrition info, ingredients, contact/QR code. Use vibrant warm gradient per product variant.
  • 5
    Product photography — invest here
    1 professional shoot for all 4 SKUs. Freshly cooked on a plate with Kerala-style props (banana leaf, copper vessel). These photos will be used everywhere. Budget: ₹3,000–₹8,000 for a local food photographer in Kozhikode.

07 🚚
Distribution & Sales
How Bite a Bite reaches every kitchen.
Week 4–8
▼
🗺️
Channel Priority Order: Start local retail → WhatsApp/Instagram D2C → then online marketplaces. Don't try all channels at once. Master one, then add.
Phase 1 Channels (Month 1–3)
  • 1
    Local Kirana & Supermarkets — Kottakkal area (20 stores)
    Walk in personally with samples. Offer consignment basis (they pay after sale) for first 2 months — this gets you shelf space without resistance. Target: Big Bazaar, local supermarkets, neighbourhood stores near colleges/PG areas.
  • 2
    Instagram DMs + WhatsApp ordering
    Your Instagram page IS your D2C storefront at start. Accept orders via DM/WhatsApp, deliver via Swiggy Genie or Dunzo or self-delivery within 10km radius. Cash on delivery or UPI. No website needed yet.
  • 3
    Cloud kitchens / Canteens / Tiffin services
    B2B sell to local cloud kitchens and office canteens who need ready-to-fry snacks. Lower margin but bulk volume. 20 packs/week to 10 canteens = 200 packs/week steady order.
Phase 2 Channels (Month 4–6)
  • 4
    Swiggy Instamart / Zepto / Blinkit (Quick Commerce)
    Kerala has strong quick-commerce penetration in Kochi, Thrissur, Kozhikode. Apply as a seller — they handle cold delivery. High visibility for frozen food. Requires FSSAI + proper labeling.
  • 5
    Your own website (Shopify, ₹2,000/month)
    Launch a simple Shopify store with 4 products. Integrate Shiprocket for pan-Kerala shipping. Enable frozen food shipping with insulated packaging + ice packs. Add WhatsApp chat widget.
⚠️
Frozen food shipping challenge: You'll need insulated box + dry ice/gel packs for deliveries over 2 hours. This adds ₹30–₹50 per order in packaging cost. Factor into shipping charges or free delivery threshold.

09 📈
Competitive Landscape & Growth
Monitor, adapt, and scale Bite a Bite.
Month 3 onwards
▼
Key Risks to Watch
🌡️ Cold Chain Failure
Product spoilage in transit = brand damage. Invest in proper packaging for delivery from Day 1.
📋 FSSAI Delay
You cannot legally sell without license. Apply Day 1. Don't sell even 1 unit without it.
📦 Inventory vs Demand Mismatch
Frozen food has limited shelf life. Make small batches (1 week stock max) until demand is predictable.
👩‍🍳 Taste Inconsistency
Your biggest asset is taste. Document recipes exactly. Weigh every ingredient. Don't wing it.
💸 Undercapitalisation
Equipment + licenses + packaging + marketing = ₹4–6L before first sale. Plan this cash flow.
🏪 Retail Shelf Rejection
Many stores won't take new brands. Start consignment. Get reorders before asking for paid placement.
KPIs to Track Monthly
  • 1
    Units produced vs units sold (sell-through rate)
    Target: 85%+ sell-through. Below 70% = overproduction problem. Adjust batch sizes weekly.
  • 2
    Cost per new customer (CPC)
    Track how much you spend on ads/samples to acquire 1 buying customer. Target under ₹150 per customer.
  • 3
    Repeat purchase rate
    After 4 weeks, what % of first-time buyers ordered again? Target 40%+. Low repeat = taste or price problem.
